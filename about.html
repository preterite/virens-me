About VIRENS Development

This is the development blog for VIRENS, an open-source research workflow system for humanities scholars. Here you’ll find build notes, design decisions, debugging stories, and the occasional piece of documentary fiction—everything that happens in the space between having an idea for a tool and watching it work.

VIRENS connects Obsidian, Bookends, DEVONthink, and a constellation of macOS automation tools into a coherent scholarly practice. This blog documents how that system gets built: the architectural choices that shape it, the frictions that refine it, and the theoretical commitments that make it more than a collection of scripts.

What You’ll Find Here

Posts are organized by the kind of story they tell, not the technology they touch. Each category names a move—a type of development work that recurs across the project.

Architecture

Structural decisions, system design

The choices that shape what everything else can become. Why three sites instead of one. Why plain text. Why modularity matters more than integration.

Build

Making something specific

A font subsetting pipeline, a layer kit generator, a bibliography script. Posts where the deliverable is a working thing that didn’t exist before.

Bridge

Theory meets implementation

Where Foucault’s panopticon informs a dashboard design, or Marxian infrastructure theory explains a CSS architecture. The posts that make VIRENS distinctive.

Friction

Things that broke, debugging stories

SVG corruption, encoding disasters, the animation that ate all its own texture. What went wrong, why, and what the fix taught us about the system.

Practice

Documentary fiction, narrative mode

Stories following Dr. Vera Green through a semester of scholarly work with VIRENS. Fiction grounded in real workflows, real frustrations, real breakthroughs.

Refinement

Iterating on working things

Second passes, performance tuning, the difference between “it works” and “it works well.” The quiet work that separates a prototype from a tool.

Topics like CSS, JavaScript, Python, Obsidian, and automation appear as tags across all categories. Browse by tag on the Tags page, or by category on the Categories page.

Who Writes This

Mike Edwards — Assistant Professor of English, Washington State University. Rhetoric and composition, digital humanities, heterodox economics. Building VIRENS because the tools available to humanities scholars assume that research is a solitary, linear process—and it isn’t.
